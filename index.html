UFC partners with Meta to stream fights through Virtual Reality

Dana White recently had the APEX facility (A Facility that hosts MMA fights for the UFC) entirely shut down to invite Mark Zuckerberg and his squad. The reason for this is because Meta will be partnering with the UFC to stream fights through VR. The first time this will be available will be during the LFA 144 event October 16th at the Sanford Pentagon in Sioux Falls. A 16-fight card that also streams on the UFC Fight Pass digital network.

Technology Enhanced Players

The future of technology used in football is here. A vast amount of new high tech equipment is here. Such as, heart rate monitors, GPS trackers on vests, advanced monitoring, camera systems, drones and many more. All these various types of equipment produces a wealthy amount of information for football players and their trainers. For example a players muscle fatigue or the evolution of his performance throughout the season. Once the info is collected and analyzed, the enhancement starts by helping to optimise training schedules and develop game strategies that fit the abilities of the player.

Vibration Sensors on Basketball Rims

Vibration sensors are placed on the backboard or rims of baskteball hoops. These sensors pick up data everytime the ball hits the rim. These statistics compile info such as launch angle, reaction time, body position, rebound angle and more. This is obviously a phenomenal piece of tech that Basketball teams use when training as teams can train how to react to a given shot. In addition to this there are different software companies that are developing algorythms that take into account different playing styles and make simulations as real as possible.
